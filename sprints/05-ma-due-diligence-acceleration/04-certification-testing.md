# Sprint 05, Task 04: Certification & Testing Strategy
## M&A Due Diligence Research Acceleration

### Objective

Research certification pathways, testing methodologies, and validation requirements for: AI-Powered Due Diligence Research Platform serving investment banks and M&A advisory firms

### Research Questions

1. What are the required security certifications for financial services (SOC 2 Type 2, ISO 27001)?
2. What testing methodologies validate accuracy of AI-generated due diligence reports?
3. What compliance certifications are needed for handling M&A transaction data?
4. What are the audit and testing requirements for financial analysis tools?
5. How should multi-source data validation be tested (3+ authoritative sources)?
6. What quality assurance processes ensure consistency across deals?
7. What user acceptance testing is required with M&A professionals?
8. What are the timeline and costs for security certifications?
9. What precedents exist for AI-powered financial tools passing regulatory scrutiny?
10. What ongoing monitoring and testing is required post-certification?

### Expected Deliverables

- Security certification requirements and pathways (SOC 2, ISO 27001) (2-3 files)
- Testing methodologies for AI accuracy and reliability (1-2 files)
- Financial services compliance requirements (FINRA, SEC) (1-2 files)
- Data validation and quality assurance processes (1-2 files)
- User acceptance testing protocols with M&A professionals (1 file)
- Certification timeline and budget estimates (1 file)
- Synthesis document with certification roadmap (1 file)

### Success Criteria

- Minimum 5 research files created
- All required certifications identified with clear pathways
- Testing methodologies aligned with financial services standards
- Validation processes ensure 3+ source cross-referencing
- Timeline realistic (SOC 2 Type 1 in 3-4 months, Type 2 in 6-9 months)
- Budget estimates grounded in market rates

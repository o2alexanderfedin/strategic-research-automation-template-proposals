# Sprint 05, Task 05: Implementation Roadmap
## M&A Due Diligence Research Acceleration

### Objective

Develop detailed implementation roadmap, partnership strategy, and go-to-market plan for: AI-Powered Due Diligence Research Platform targeting investment banks and M&A advisory firms

### Research Questions

1. What is the optimal phased roadmap from MVP to enterprise-ready platform?
2. What are the key milestones for each phase (Product Development, Pilot, Case Studies, Sales Launch, Scale)?
3. What partnerships are critical (deal management systems, financial data providers, M&A associations)?
4. What is the pilot program structure (2-3 firms, 1-2 deals each, free/discounted)?
5. What customer acquisition strategies are most effective (direct outreach, thought leadership, referrals)?
6. What are the resource requirements (team, budget, timeline) for each phase?
7. What metrics define success at each phase (time savings, cost reduction, analyst satisfaction)?
8. What risks exist at each phase and how should they be mitigated?
9. What customer success practices ensure 90%+ renewal rates?
10. What international expansion strategy follows US market validation?

### Expected Deliverables

- Phased implementation roadmap (5 phases over 18-36 months) (1-2 files)
- Partnership strategy and target partner list (1-2 files)
- Pilot program design and success criteria (1-2 files)
- Go-to-market strategy (customer acquisition, thought leadership) (1-2 files)
- Resource planning (team hiring, budget allocation) (1 file)
- Success metrics and KPIs by phase (1 file)
- Risk mitigation strategies (1 file)
- Customer success and renewal strategy (1 file)
- Synthesis document with roadmap overview (1 file)

### Success Criteria

- Minimum 6 research files created
- Roadmap realistic with achievable milestones
- Partnership strategy identifies 3-5 critical partners
- Pilot program structure validated against M&A industry norms
- Go-to-market strategy aligned with customer buying cycles (Q4 planning)
- Resource requirements feasible for startup execution
